Patchnotes

  • Slugging & Recovery Changes: Survivors can now crawl and recover at the same time. Recovery is automatic and crawl speed increases over time. Tap Interact when full to pick yourself up.
  • Tunneling Penalties: If a Survivor dies before reaching 6 total hooks, the remaining Survivors gain a 25% generator repair speed bonus. Killers lose the ability to block, damage, or regress generators after a Survivor is sacrificed.
  • Map Updates: Pallet distribution and dead-zones adjusted across multiple Realms to improve loop balance and reduce “no-escape” areas.
  • The Shape (Michael Myers) Rework: Now features three modes — Stalker, Pursuer, and Evil Incarnate. Evil Incarnate unlocks new special attacks (like “Slaughtering Strike”) and improved vault/break speeds.

Spotlight

Survivor of the Week – Meg Thomas

Meg Thomas is a high-energy athlete whose perks focus on speed, stealth, and clutch escapes. She’s perfect for players who like to rush objectives and outmaneuver killers.

  • Quick & Quiet: Vault over obstacles and enter lockers quickly and silently, suppressing loud noise notifications every 30 seconds (reduced cooldown at higher perk tiers).
  • Sprint Burst: Gain a burst of speed (150%) for 3 seconds when you start running. Causes the Exhausted status effect for 60/50/40 seconds.
  • Adrenaline: Instantly heal one health state and sprint at 150% speed for 5 seconds when the Exit Gates are powered. Works even if you’re hooked or downed (but will put you into the Exhausted state after use).

Spotlight: Killer of the Week – The Huntress

Armed with a deadly set of throwing hatchets, The Huntress excels at mid-range pressure and zoning Survivors. Mastering her lullaby and hatchet arcs makes her one of the scariest killers in the Fog.

  • Beast of Prey: Gain the Undetectable status effect when Bloodlust activates and earn bonus Bloodpoints for chasing Survivors.
  • Territorial Imperative: Reveal the aura of Survivors who enter the basement while you are at least 32 meters away.
  • Hex: Huntress Lullaby: Survivors receive a 2% regression penalty on missed skill checks, and as the Hex grows stronger, skill check warning sounds become shorter until they are completely removed.
