Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd President of the United States.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence and later the 2nd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Celebrated diplomat, inventor, and elder statesman of the American Revolution.
John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress at the time, known for his large, bold signature.
Samuel Adams
Revolutionary leader and organizer of protests like the Boston Tea Party.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four of America’s founding documents: the Declaration of Independence, Articles of Association, Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Charles Carroll
The only Catholic signer of the Declaration of Independence and one of the wealthiest signers.

The Declaration of Independence was not only a bold statement of freedom but also a testament to the vision and courage of its signers. Figures like Thomas Jefferson, John Adams, Benjamin Franklin, John Hancock, and Samuel Adams helped shape the ideals that still define the United States today. Their signatures symbolize far more than ink on parchment—they represent the principles of liberty, unity, and perseverance that continue to inspire generations.
