The Rulebook

What is the Rulebook

The so-called “Survivor Rulebook” for killers isn’t an official set of rules from Behaviour Interactive. Instead, it’s a term used by the Dead by Daylight community to describe a list of unwritten expectations or “house rules” that some Survivors believe Killers should follow. Over time it has led to debate and even division within the DBD community about which rules, if any, should be followed.

This “rulebook” has also been a source of toxicity from both sides: Survivors accusing Killers of being unfair, and Killers pushing back against being told how to play. At the end of the day, Dead by Daylight is meant to be a tense but enjoyable experience for everyone. Nothing beats good sportsmanship, and while the game can be tough, the goal should always be to have fun.

Below are a few personal guidelines I like to follow as Killer to keep matches fun and fair for both sides:

  1. Don’t camp hooks unless absolutely necessary.
  2. Avoid tunneling the same Survivor right off hook if there are other targets nearby.
  3. Mix up chase targets to give everyone a chance to play.
  4. Give the last Survivor a chance at the hatch once in a while.
  5. Respect great plays — if a Survivor pulls off something amazing, let them have their moment!
